# User Research & Personas

## Overview

These personas and scenarios are based on user research with costume designers and creative professionals. They guide all product decisions and validate implementation success.

**Purpose:** Keep user needs at the center of every implementation decision. When in doubt about a feature, ask: "Does this help Sarah update from her phone?" or "Would Marcus understand this without instructions?"

---

## Primary Personas

### Sarah Chen - Theatre Costume Designer

**Background:**
- Works on multiple productions simultaneously (typically 2-3 shows at different stages)
- 8+ years experience in theatre costume design
- Tech-comfortable but not a developer - uses iPhone fluently but gets frustrated with complex software
- Portfolio is critical for pitching to directors and producers
- Works in fast-paced theatrical environments with tight deadlines

**Needs:**
- Showcase work to directors and producers with professional presentation
- Quick updates between productions (often same-day turnaround needed)
- Mobile-first workflow (often on set/backstage during rehearsals)
- Ability to pivot presentation for different audiences (classical vs contemporary)
- Fast, reliable publishing - no time for debugging

**Pain Points:**
- Current portfolio solutions don't work well on mobile - forced to wait until home
- Desktop-only editors mean missing opportunities to share fresh work immediately
- Takes photos on iPhone but can't upload them until at her laptop
- Theatre WiFi is often slow and unreliable
- Needs something that "just works" without technical troubleshooting

**Success Metric:** Can add production photos from phone in <5 minutes, even on poor WiFi

**Technology Context:**
- Primary device: iPhone 14 Pro (takes all her work photos on this)
- Secondary device: MacBook Pro (uses occasionally at home)
- Network: Theatre WiFi (often slow/unreliable), mobile data (limited)
- Tech comfort: High with consumer apps, low patience for complex software

**Typical Use Cases:**
- Updates portfolio during dress rehearsals to show director
- Adds photos between performances during production runs
- Reorganizes content before meetings with new clients
- Shares portfolio link via text message immediately after shoots

## Core Success Scenarios

### Scenario 1: First Portfolio Creation (Marcus)

**User:** Marcus Williams (new freelancer)

**Context:** Marcus just finished his first professional photoshoot with a local fashion photographer. He has 10 high-quality images and needs a portfolio website by tomorrow for a client meeting. He's never built a website before and is worried about looking unprofessional.

**Success Criteria:** Creates and publishes a professional portfolio in under 30 minutes that he's confident sharing with clients.

**Journey:**
1. Marcus discovers the portfolio builder through a colleague's recommendation
2. He signs up and is welcomed with a clear starting point (not overwhelmed)
3. He selects a theme that matches his aesthetic vision (modern, minimal)
4. He adds his business name "Marcus Williams Design" and tagline "Contemporary Fashion Design"
5. He uploads 10 photos from his recent shoot
6. The system handles high-resolution images gracefully (no errors or confusion)
7. He adds brief descriptions for each piece (garment details, inspiration)
8. He previews how it will look to visitors (builds confidence)
9. He publishes his portfolio and immediately shares the link on Instagram
10. His portfolio looks professional and loads quickly on his followers' phones

**Key Moments:**
- **Theme selection** immediately shows him what's possible (visual confidence)
- **Photo upload** handles his 5MB+ images without technical errors or confusing messages
- **Preview** gives him confidence before publishing (critical for first-timer)
- **Published site** impresses his potential clients (professional result validates effort)

**Failure Indicators:**
- Gets stuck on any step for more than 2 minutes without knowing what to do
- Sees error messages that require technical knowledge to resolve
- Published result looks amateur or broken on mobile devices
- Takes longer than 30 minutes from start to shareable link

### Scenario 2: Mobile Update from Set (Sarah)

**User:** Sarah Chen (experienced costume designer)

**Context:** Sarah is backstage during dress rehearsal for a new theatre production. The lead actor just tried on the final costume and it looks perfect. She wants to add photos to her portfolio immediately to share with the director who's considering her for another show. She only has her iPhone and the theatre WiFi is spotty.

**Success Criteria:** Updates portfolio from phone in under 5 minutes, with changes live and shareable.

**Journey:**
1. Sarah takes 3-4 photos of the lead actor in costume using her iPhone camera
2. Opens the portfolio builder on her iPhone Safari browser
3. Navigates to her "Current Productions" page (easily finds it in mobile view)
4. Taps to add new images to the gallery
5. Selects photos directly from her camera roll (just captured)
6. System uploads images even on slow theatre WiFi (with progress indication)
7. Adds quick captions while details are fresh: "Juliet - Final costume, opening night ready"
8. Taps "Publish" to make changes live
9. Immediately texts the portfolio link to the director
10. Director views updated portfolio on their phone during intermission

**Key Moments:**
- **Mobile interface is fully functional**, not a stripped-down version (touch targets work perfectly)
- **Photo upload works smoothly even on poor WiFi** (handles interruptions, shows progress)
- **Can complete entire update without switching to desktop** (truly mobile-first)
- **Changes are live immediately** for the director to see (explicit publish model works quickly)

**Failure Indicators:**
- Touch targets too small or require precise tapping (frustrating on phone)
- Upload fails or hangs on slow WiFi without clear error recovery
- Interface requires desktop features (hover states, precise cursor control)
- Process takes longer than 5 minutes (misses window of opportunity)

### Scenario 3: Organizing Decades of Work (Emma)

**User:** Emma Rodriguez (veteran professional)

**Context:** Emma is migrating from her outdated website (built in 2008) to a modern portfolio. She has 50+ major productions across film, television, and theatre, each with multiple images, credits, and collaborators. She needs to organize this extensive body of work in a way that's navigable for potential clients while maintaining her professional credibility.

**Success Criteria:** Creates organized, navigable portfolio with 50+ projects that feels professional and comprehensive.

**Journey:**
1. Emma logs in and chooses a classic, elegant theme (suits her established reputation)
2. Creates main pages: Film Work, Television, Theatre, Awards & Recognition
3. Within Film Work, creates sub-pages for each major production (20+ films)
4. For each film, uploads production stills, costume sketches, and detail shots
5. Uses bulk upload to handle dozens of images efficiently (not one-by-one)
6. Adds detailed credits: director, production designer, key collaborators
7. Sets certain pages as password-protected (unreleased films under NDA)
8. Creates a curated highlights reel on the homepage (best 10-15 projects)
9. Tests password protection to ensure unreleased work is secure
10. Publishes and redirects her old domain to new portfolio
11. Navigation remains clear despite 50+ pages of content

**Key Moments:**
- **Page organization supports complex categorization** (multi-level hierarchy works)
- **Bulk upload handles dozens of images efficiently** (not tedious one-by-one)
- **Password protection gives control over sensitive content** (NDA compliance)
- **Navigation remains clear despite extensive content** (doesn't feel overwhelming to visitors)

**Failure Indicators:**
- Can't organize projects into logical categories (flat structure only)
- Bulk upload fails or requires one-by-one uploads (too tedious)
- Password protection is confusing or unreliable
- Navigation becomes cluttered or confusing with many pages

### Scenario 4: Quick Theme Change for Different Audience (Sarah)

**User:** Sarah Chen (theatre costume designer)

**Context:** Sarah typically works on classical theatre (Shakespeare, period dramas) but has a meeting in 30 minutes with a contemporary dance company. Her portfolio currently features her classical work with an elegant, traditional theme. She needs to quickly adapt the presentation to appeal to the modern dance aesthetic without rebuilding everything.

**Success Criteria:** Adapts portfolio presentation in under 10 minutes to match audience expectations.

**Journey:**
1. Sarah has a video call meeting in 30 minutes with contemporary dance artistic director
2. Logs into portfolio builder on her MacBook
3. Switches from "Classic Elegant" theme to "Modern Minimal" theme
4. Reorders her homepage to feature contemporary work first (drag-and-drop reordering)
5. Temporarily hides her Shakespeare and period drama pages from navigation
6. Updates her homepage tagline: "Costume Design for Movement & Innovation"
7. Previews the changes to ensure cohesive visual presentation
8. Publishes the targeted version
9. Opens portfolio during video call, confidently showing aligned work
10. After meeting, switches back to classical theme in 2 minutes

**Key Moments:**
- **Theme switch preserves all content and structure** (just changes presentation)
- **Can quickly reorganize without rebuilding** (drag-and-drop is fast)
- **Preview confirms new look works** (builds confidence before meeting)
- **Publishing is fast enough for last-minute adjustments** (not a 10-minute build process)

**Failure Indicators:**
- Theme switch breaks layouts or loses content (user fears changing themes)
- Reorganization is too slow or requires rebuilding pages
- Can't temporarily hide content (must delete and re-add)
- Changes take too long to publish (misses meeting window)

### Scenario 5: Collaborative Review Process (Marcus)

**User:** Marcus Williams (freelance fashion designer)

**Context:** Marcus has completed his first portfolio draft but is nervous about publishing it. His mentor (an established designer) offered to review it before it goes live. Marcus needs to share the work-in-progress for feedback without making it public, then iterate based on suggestions.

**Success Criteria:** Shares work for review without publishing publicly, iterates quickly based on feedback.

**Journey:**
1. Marcus completes his portfolio draft (feels 90% ready)
2. Generates a preview link with 48-hour expiration (doesn't want it public forever)
3. Sends preview link to his mentor via email
4. Mentor opens link on iPad, reviews during lunch break
5. Mentor suggests reordering pieces: "Lead with the editorial work, it's your strongest"
6. They schedule a quick video call to discuss
7. Marcus makes adjustments in real-time during their video call (reorders gallery)
8. Shares screen so mentor can see changes immediately (or refreshes preview link)
9. After mentor approval, Marcus feels confident
10. Publishes portfolio with explicit "Publish" action (knows it's now live)

**Key Moments:**
- **Preview link works without requiring mentor to log in** (no friction for reviewer)
- **Real-time editing enables collaborative review** (can make changes during call)
- **Changes are visible immediately in preview** (mentor sees updates)
- **Publishing after review feels safe and confident** (explicit action, not accidental)

**Failure Indicators:**
- Preview requires login or complex access setup (friction prevents review)
- Can't edit while preview is active (must close preview, edit, regenerate)
- Changes don't show up in preview without complex refresh
- Unclear if portfolio is public or private (accidental publishing)

## Validation Framework

### After Each Implementation Slice

Before marking a slice as "complete," validate against these user-centric questions:

#### Marcus Questions (First-Time User)

**Approachability:**
- Can he figure out where to start without reading documentation?
- Is the interface self-explanatory or does it require prior knowledge?
- Are error messages helpful rather than technical?
- Does he feel confident or confused?

**Professional Result:**
- Would the result look professional enough to share with clients?
- Does it load quickly on mobile devices?
- Are there obvious visual bugs or layout issues?
- Would he be proud to put his name on this?

**Speed:**
- Can he complete core tasks in under 30 minutes?
- Are there frustrating delays or loading times?
- Does the workflow feel efficient or tedious?

#### Sarah Questions (Mobile-First)

**Mobile Completeness:**
- Can she complete the entire task on her iPhone alone (not just view)?
- Are touch targets large enough for easy tapping?
- Does the interface work well on iPhone Safari (primary browser)?
- Can she use it while standing backstage (not just sitting at desk)?

**Network Resilience:**
- Does it work well on poor WiFi (theatre/set conditions)?
- Does it handle upload interruptions gracefully?
- Are there clear progress indicators for slow connections?
- Can she recover from network errors without losing work?

**Speed:**
- Is the flow fast enough for backstage updates (<5 minutes)?
- Does publishing happen quickly enough for immediate sharing?
- Are there unnecessary steps that slow her down?

#### Emma Questions (Power User)

**Organization:**
- Can she organize complex content structures (50+ projects)?
- Does the navigation remain usable with lots of content?
- Can she categorize work in multiple ways?
- Does bulk upload work efficiently?

**Advanced Features:**
- Are advanced features discoverable without being overwhelming?
- Does password protection work reliably?
- Can she share specific pages (not just entire portfolio)?
- Does the system handle large portfolios performantly?

**Control:**
- Does she have enough control over organization and presentation?
- Can she migrate existing content without starting over?
- Does preview work for complex portfolios?

### Success Indicators

✅ **User completes task without frustration** - natural flow, no confusion  
✅ **Result meets professional quality standards** - looks polished, not amateur  
✅ **Time requirements met** - <30 min creation, <5 min mobile update  
✅ **User expresses confidence in result** - proud to share, not embarrassed  
✅ **Works on real devices** - tested on actual iPhone/Android, not just desktop  
✅ **Handles real content** - large images, lots of text, realistic complexity  

### Failure Indicators

❌ **User gets stuck or confused** - unclear what to do next  
❌ **Requires desktop when mobile should work** - mobile features missing  
❌ **Result looks amateur or broken** - visible bugs, poor layouts  
❌ **Performance degrades with realistic content** - slow with many images  
❌ **Error handling is poor** - confusing error messages, lost work  
❌ **Requires technical knowledge** - users shouldn't need to understand technical concepts

## Edge Cases from User Research

Real-world scenarios that must be handled gracefully:

### Content Volume Edge Cases

**Large Uploads:**
- User uploads 100+ images at once (Emma migrating from old site)
- Single gallery with 50+ images (Emma's major film project)
- Portfolio with 20+ pages (Emma's complete career)

**Expected Behavior:**
- Batch processing for large uploads (not all at once)
- Progress indicators for long operations
- Performance doesn't degrade with content volume
- Clear limits communicated before operations

### Network Conditions Edge Cases

**Poor Connectivity:**
- Poor WiFi on theatre set (Sarah's context) - 0.5-1 Mbps
- Mobile data with limited bandwidth (Sarah's context) - 3G speeds
- Intermittent connection (drops during upload)

**Expected Behavior:**
- Upload interruption recovery (resume, not restart)
- Optimistic UI updates (feels fast even on slow connection)
- Clear network status indication
- Graceful degradation (core features work, nice-to-haves defer)

### User Error Edge Cases

**Accidental Actions:**
- Accidental content deletion (panic recovery needed)
- Session expires while editing (work not saved)
- Browser crash mid-edit (auto-save recovery)
- Switch between devices while editing (sync issues)

**Expected Behavior:**
- Undo/redo for destructive actions
- Auto-save with clear saved status indication
- Recovery mechanism for interrupted sessions
- Clear conflict resolution for multi-device editing

### Content Type Edge Cases

**File Handling:**
- Unsupported file types uploaded (graceful rejection with explanation)
- Very large images (>10MB originals, >5000px dimensions)
- Corrupted image files (handle without crashing)
- Missing required information (prevent publish, clear errors)

**Expected Behavior:**
- Clear error messages for unsupported types (suggest alternatives)
- Automatic optimization for large images (with user notification)
- Validation prevents publishing incomplete content
- Helpful error messages guide user to resolution

### Workflow Edge Cases

**State Transitions:**
- Theme switch with incompatible components (migration path needed)
- Publish with validation failures (clear, actionable errors)
- Password-protected content management (Emma's need for NDA compliance)
- Preview link expiration (clear communication)

**Expected Behavior:**
- Theme switches preserve content (layout adapts)
- Validation errors block publish with clear fix instructions
- Password protection reliably prevents access
- Expired preview links show clear expiration message

## How to Use This Document

### For Design Decisions

**Reference personas when deciding on features:**
- "Would this help Sarah update from her phone?"
- "Would Marcus understand this without instructions?"
- "Does Emma need this level of control?"

**Prioritize by user impact:**
- Sarah's mobile needs are critical (core value proposition)
- Marcus's ease-of-use is essential (first-time user success)
- Emma's advanced features are important but can come later

### For Implementation

**Use scenarios as acceptance criteria:**
- Can you demonstrate Scenario 2 working end-to-end on an actual iPhone?
- Does Scenario 1 complete in under 30 minutes with realistic content?
- Can you show Scenario 3 handling 50+ projects without performance issues?

**Test with realistic profiles:**
- Use pre-seeded accounts (not empty test accounts)
- Test with actual image sizes and quantities users will have
- Validate on real devices (iPhone, Android), not just browser dev tools

### For Testing

**Validate each slice against personas:**
- After implementing image upload: Test with Sarah's iPhone on slow WiFi
- After implementing galleries: Test with Emma's 50-image production gallery
- After implementing onboarding: Test with Marcus's first-time user perspective

**Use validation framework questions:**
- Run through Marcus/Sarah/Emma questions after each slice
- Document answers (not just "yes/no" but actual observations)
- Identify friction points and prioritize fixes

### For Validation

**Demo with persona scenarios:**
- Record demos showing each scenario working
- Use test accounts that match persona contexts
- Show realistic content and workflows (not toy examples)

**Validate success indicators:**
- Measure actual time to complete tasks
- Test on real devices in realistic conditions
- Gather feedback: "Would you be confident sharing this?"
